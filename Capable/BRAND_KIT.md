## Brand Overview

Capable is a Vibe coding platform designed to help developers build and validate MVPs quickly. We believe the best way to know if a project is worth building at scale is to test it first—cheap, fast, and with minimal overhead.

Our brand reflects this philosophy: modern, practical, efficient, and confident. We speak directly to developers, respect their intelligence, and deliver value without the bloat.

---

## Mission, Vision & Values

### Mission
To empower developers to validate ideas quickly, without wasting time or resources on over-engineering.

### Vision
A world where every great idea gets tested before it gets built—reducing risk, accelerating learning, and unlocking innovation.

**Speed:** Fast iteration beats perfection. Ship early, learn faster.

**Pragmatism:** Build what works, not what's impressive. Real solutions over feature bloat.

**Confidence:** We believe in developers, and in the power of validation. We know the pain points and have solutions.

**Approachability:** Expert knowledge delivered without elitism. We're here to help.

## Typography

### Font Family

**Primary: Arial**

Used for all headings, body text, and UI elements. Arial is universally available, highly legible, and conveys a modern, professional tone. This ensures consistency across web, desktop, print, and presentations.

### Typographic Hierarchy

| Level | Size | Weight | Color | Use Case |
|-------|------|--------|-------|----------|
| **H1** | 36pt | Bold | #1F4788 | Page titles, hero sections, main headings |
| **H2** | 28pt | Bold | #2E5FA3 | Section headings, major subsections |
| **H3** | 24pt | Bold | #4A7BC8 | Subsection headings, UI headers |
| **Body** | 14-16pt | Regular | #1F2937 | Paragraphs, descriptions, standard text |
| **Small** | 12pt | Regular | #6B7280 | Captions, labels, fine print, metadata |

### Line Spacing

- Headings: 1.2x line height
- Body text: 1.5x to 1.6x line height (improves readability)
- Paragraphs: 1.5x line height minimum for web

### Letter Spacing

- Headings: Normal (0)
- Body: Normal (0)
- UI Labels: +0.5px (subtle emphasis without distortion)

## Voice & Tone

### Tone Principles

**Be Direct.** Say what you mean. Avoid jargon and corporate speak. Developers respect straight talk.

**Be Confident.** We know the problem and have a solution. Don't apologize for being different.

**Be Helpful.** Show developers how to get value, not just what features exist. Lead with results.

**Be Human.** Write like you're talking to a friend, not reading a manual. Use contractions, casual language where appropriate.

**Be Honest.** Admit limitations. Show the real value, not hype. Trust the product to speak for itself.

### Examples

#### Say This...
- "Build your MVP in days, not months."
- "Test your idea before you commit."
- "Start here, iterate fast, validate early."
- "Stop over-engineering. Ship something real."
- "Validate fast, fail fast, learn faster."

#### Not This...
- "Synergize your deployment paradigm."
- "Leverage cutting-edge rapid prototyping infrastructure."
- "Empower your cross-functional enablement strategy."
- "Unlock next-generation innovation potential."
- "Enterprise-grade solution architecture."

### Tone Across Contexts

**Marketing/Website:** Bold, confident, inspiring. Show the promise without hype.

**In-App UI:** Clear, instructional, encouraging. Guide the user without talking down.

**Errors:** Helpful, not blaming. Explain what went wrong and how to fix it.

**Documentation:** Direct and practical. Answer the "how" before the "why."

**Social Media:** Casual, relatable, real. Show the person behind the product.

## Logo & Visual Identity

### Logo Usage

- Always use the official Capable logo—never recreate, distort, or modify it
- Maintain clear space around the logo (minimum 20px on all sides)
- Use full-color logo on light backgrounds (Navy Blue with mark)
- Use white logo on dark backgrounds
- Minimum size: 64px on screen, 0.5 inch in print
- Never place the logo on a gradient or busy background

### Visual Elements

#### Grid & Spacing
Use an 8px base grid system for consistency. All spacing, padding, and margins should be multiples of 8px (8px, 16px, 24px, 32px, etc.). This creates visual harmony and makes scaling across devices easier.

#### Corners
Use subtle rounded corners (4-8px border-radius) on UI components. Keep corners sharp on text and data-heavy areas. The overall aesthetic should feel modern but not playful.

#### Icons
- Use consistent, minimal line-based icons
- Icon weight should match body text (1.5-2px stroke weight)
- Recommended libraries: Feather Icons, Heroicons, Material Design Icons
- All icons should have clear negative space and be recognizable at small sizes

#### Imagery
- Prefer clean, modern product screenshots over generic stock photos
- Use real developer hands, real code, real workflows
- High contrast and consistent lighting across all images
- Illustrations should be minimal, purposeful, and on-brand (not cartoonish)
- Photography should feel authentic, not staged

#### Patterns & Textures
Keep patterns subtle. Never use busy or distracting backgrounds. White space is our most valuable design element.

## Brand Applications

### Website

- **Hero Section:** Navy Blue background, white text, clear CTA button
- **Body Backgrounds:** White or Light Gray (#F3F4F6)
- **Buttons:** Navy Blue (primary), Light Blue text on white background with border (secondary)
- **Links:** Light Blue (#4A7BC8), underlined, should clearly indicate interactivity
- **Ample whitespace:** Never clutter the design. Let content breathe.

### Application UI

- **Primary Buttons:** Navy Blue background, white text, rounded corners (6-8px)
- **Secondary Buttons:** Light Blue text on Light Gray background with border
- **Tertiary/Text Buttons:** Navy Blue text, no background (hover adds Light Gray background)
- **Success States:** Use Success Green (#10D981), checkmark icon
- **Errors:** Use Error Red (#EF4444), clear error message, actionable fix suggestion
- **Warnings:** Use Warning Amber (#F59E0B), informational icon
- **Disabled States:** Light Gray text, Light Gray background, 50% opacity

### Email

- **Email Headers:** Navy Blue background (#1F4788), white text, Capable logo left-aligned
- **CTA Buttons:** Navy Blue background, white text, center-aligned
- **Email Footer:** Medium Gray text on white background, Light Gray top border

### Marketing Materials

- **Presentations:** Title slides use Navy Blue background with white text
- **Social Media:** Use brand colors boldly, high contrast product imagery, direct messaging
- **Print (Business Cards, Letterhead):** Navy Blue accent, simple, professional
- **Advertisements:** Clean layout, strong typography, clear value proposition

## Do's & Don'ts

### Do

✓ Keep the design clean and minimal—whitespace is your ally

✓ Use the color palette consistently across all touchpoints

✓ Write in a direct, helpful voice in all copy

✓ Prioritize clarity over creativity—a confused user is a lost user

✓ Use whitespace generously to guide the eye and improve readability

✓ Test all designs for accessibility (contrast, keyboard navigation, screen readers)

✓ Maintain consistent rounded corners, spacing, and typography

✓ Use Navy Blue as the dominant brand color

✓ Ask yourself: "Does this serve the user or just look pretty?"

### Don't

✗ Don't alter, distort, or recreate the Capable logo

✗ Don't use colors outside the approved palette

✗ Don't use decorative, ornate fonts (Arial only, please)

✗ Don't add unnecessary complexity to designs or copy

✗ Don't use generic stock photos that feel clichéd

✗ Don't make promises the product can't deliver

✗ Don't ignore accessibility standards (contrast, readability, keyboard nav)

✗ Don't use animated GIFs or auto-playing videos without user permission

✗ Don't mix brand guidelines from different projects or past branding

✗ Don't assume—test designs and copy with real users
